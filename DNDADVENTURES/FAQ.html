What kind of die should I use when I roll?

90% of the time, you will be using a 20-sided die or d20. any time you make an attack roll, an ability check, a skill check, or a saving throw, you will be using a 20-sided die. you will likely be using other types of dice for damage rolls, and determing how many hit points you gain when you rest or level up.

What does 4d6, Etc. mean?

When you are rolling something other than the 20-sided die (d20) the game will likely have you roll several dice of the same kind and add the results. the first number refers to how many dice you will be rolling, and the second number refers to what kind of dice you will be rolling. So 4d6 means to roll 4 6-sided dice and add them together.

What are ability scores and ability modifiers?

Ability scores are the physical and mental capabilities of your character. a character has six ability scores. Strength, which determines you physical strength, dexterity, which determines how fast and agile you character is, constitution determines how tough your character is, intelligence determines how smart and educated your character is. Wisdom determines how much common sense and awareness you character has, and charisma determines how persuasive your character is.

Your ability scores determine your ability modifiers. You do some simple math to convert an ability score to the corresponding ability modifier. You add your ability modifier to any d20 die rolls that are related to that ability. For example, you would add your strength modifier to a roll to see if you can lift a heavy rock, and you would add your intelligence modifier to a d20 roll to determine of you can remember something.

Overview

Purpose

Think about the last time you played a D&D game. How hard was it to schedule? Did everyone understand the rules or where they could find them? How hard was finding players? Did the game go smoothly? Solving those problems is where my website comes in. I provide a scheduling tool that has a concise scheduling calendar, links to rules and resources that are useful to any game, and room to include any house rules that you want for your table.

Audience

My target audience is college age young adults who either have experience with DND, or complete novices. One of the biggest issues of playing a DND game is finding a local group to join. The need I am providing is a system for organizing DND sessions, as well as resources for rules, play, and gamemastering. My audience will access my website from desktop, laptop, and mobile devices that means that my website will need to be adjusted for all three devices.

Content

Home page

scheduling

Dungeons and dragons is an incredibly fun game. While frequently derided as “a game for nerds,” the fact that it requires several different people to talk and interact with each other shows that is not true. DnD is a game that can be enjoyed by everyone. However, there are several problems that need to be overcome for DnD to be enjoyed.

The first problem is scheduling. DnD sessions can take several hours, and a campaign requires several sessions that requires all the same people to be available at the same time. This makes it very difficult to get a game going because it’s very difficult to make sure everyone is available, and if one person can’t make it, the others either can’t play, or they need to find a way to do without the missing person. My website will make it easier to schedule by having everyone in a group put the days they are available on a calendar so they can deal with any scheduling problems in advance.

Player resources

The second problem is that DnD is a very complex game with several books’ worth of rules. Many players start with barely any idea of what their character is capable of beyond their level, armor class, and hit points. Wizards of the coast has helped to make it easier by making many of the basic rules of the game free to anyone with an internet connection, and many other people help provide useful advice to players and Dungeon Masters both. The problem is that many players don’t know where to find these resources or that the exist at all. My website will have internet hyperlinks leading to the resources I’ve found so players won’t have to hunt them down and I won't have to repeat what several other people have already said.

FAQ

The third problem is that some parts of the game simply will not be clear because the authors thought it was obvious. To deal with that issue, my website will have a Frequently Asked Questions page or FAQ that will answer questions such as, ‘what is a proficiency bonus?’ ‘what dice do I roll?’ ‘What does 5d6 mean?’ as well as other questions that newer players will have.
